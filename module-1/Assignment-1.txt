Ethereum Overview

Objective: The objective of this assignment is to provide students with a fundamental understanding of Ethereum, its history, objectives, and significance in the blockchain ecosystem.

Assignment Description:

Ethereum is one of the most prominent blockchain platforms, known for its smart contract capabilities and decentralized applications (DApps). This assignment aims to ensure that students have a solid foundation in Ethereum by researching and summarizing key information about the platform.

Instructions:

Research Ethereum's History and Founder:

Instruct students to research and write a concise overview of Ethereum's history, including when it was founded and the key milestones in its development.
Ask them to provide information about Ethereum's founder, Vitalik Buterin, including his background and his role in creating Ethereum.
Ethereum's Objectives and Advantages:

Task students with explaining Ethereum's primary objectives as a blockchain platform. What problems or limitations was Ethereum designed to address?
Encourage them to highlight the advantages of Ethereum's decentralized and programmable nature compared to traditional centralized systems.
Examples of Decentralized Applications (DApps):

Instruct students to provide examples of notable decentralized applications (DApps) that are built on the Ethereum platform.
Ask them to briefly describe the purpose and functionality of each DApp, showcasing the diverse range of applications Ethereum supports.
Clarity and Conciseness:

Emphasize the importance of clarity and conciseness in their write-up. The overview should be easily understandable by someone new to blockchain technology.
Citations and References:

Encourage students to cite their sources and provide references for the information they include in their overview.
Presentation:

Specify any formatting or presentation guidelines for the assignment, such as font size, margins, or document length.
Grading Criteria:

This assignment will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

Accuracy and completeness of the historical overview of Ethereum.
Clarity in explaining Ethereum's primary objectives and advantages.
Appropriateness and relevance of the chosen examples of Ethereum-based DApps.
Proper citation of sources and references.
Overall presentation and adherence to formatting guidelines.
